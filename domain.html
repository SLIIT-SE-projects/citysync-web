Domain Research

Understanding the problem and our technical approach.

Literature Survey

Traditional traffic controllers operate on static timers and struggle with the dynamic, unpredictable nature of real-world urban networks. While recent studies have explored adaptive methods, many fail to scale across large city grids or lack preemptive optimization. Our survey identified a critical need for systems combining the predictive power of Model Predictive Control (MPC) to handle local intersection complexities, integrated with the spatial-temporal awareness of Graph Neural Networks (GNN) for city-wide coordination.

Research Gap & Problem

The Gap: Existing traffic controllers lack the ability to look ahead globally across multiple intersections, and mostly ignore critical scenarios like emergency vehicle routing.

The Problem: How can we intelligently predict and optimize traffic signal timings in a dense urban grid to minimize total vehicle delay, while providing a dynamic "Green Wave" preemption system for emergency vehicles without completely disrupting regular traffic flow?

Objectives

  • Model dynamic urban traffic environments using SUMO.
  • Implement MPC to solve complex local traffic constraints and compute optimal phase durations.
  • Develop a GNN to understand spatial-temporal relationships across the entire city network.
  • Create an automated Emergency Vehicle Preemption system to clear paths for critical vehicles.
  • Build interactive Web and Mobile Interfaces for administrators and drivers.

Methodology

The system follows a microservice-oriented workflow. The Simulation Core runs SUMO and extracts real-time state data (waiting cars, EV presence). This data is fed into isolated AI background services. The GNN Optimizer and MPC Controller concurrently analyze the network state to dictate optimal signal changes. Meanwhile, the Emergency Preemption Service constantly monitors for critical vehicles to bypass normal schedules. Finally, data is channeled through a centralized API to live user dashboards.
