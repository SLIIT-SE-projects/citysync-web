Project Abstract

Urban traffic congestion is a rapidly growing global issue, leading to increased travel times, economic losses, and severe environmental impacts. Traditional traffic light systems operate on fixed timers and cannot adapt well to real-world anomalies, traffic jams, or emergency vehicles.

CitySync AI replaces static systems with a smart, data-driven framework. We utilize Model Predictive Control (MPC) and Graph Neural Networks (GNN) to predict traffic behavior and adapt signal lights dynamically across the city grid. The system also includes an automated Emergency Vehicle Preemption protocol to dynamically clear a path ("Green Wave") for critical vehicles.

Through extensive testing in SUMO (Simulation of Urban MObility), our project bridges the gap between theoretical AI models and practical, deployable smart city infrastructure, all accessible through dedicated Web and Mobile interfaces.

GNN & MPC

Predicting traffic flow and spatial-temporal relationships to preemptively adjust signal phases.

Emergency Preemption

Automatically prioritizing critical vehicles by generating dynamic "Green Waves" through the city.

SUMO Simulation

Rigorous testing and validation in highly realistic, large-scale urban micro-simulations.
